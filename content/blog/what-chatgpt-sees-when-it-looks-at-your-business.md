## A quick experiment you can do right now

Open ChatGPT. Type: "Who are the top three [your category] in [your town]?"

See what comes back.

If your business is named — congrats, you're showing up. If not, you're invisible to roughly half the customers who are starting to do this kind of search.

Now do it again with a different phrasing. "I need a reliable mover in [your town] for a small apartment." Or "Best dentist near me for nervous patients in [your zip]." Whatever your actual customers ask.

That answer — the one that comes back, with or without your name in it — that's the picture ChatGPT has of your business and your local market right now.

## ChatGPT doesn't "know" you. It assembles you.

Here's the part most owners don't realize: ChatGPT doesn't have a private file on your business. It doesn't have a database of every local company. It builds its answer in real time, by pulling from a handful of sources, and then writes that answer in plain English.

The sources it pulls from are roughly:

- Your Google Business Profile information (hours, services, address, reviews)
- Your website's text, if it's findable and clearly written
- Mentions of your business on directories, news sites, and other people's websites
- Review content — what customers actually said about you
- Sometimes, things you've written that have been indexed by Google or Bing

If those sources tell a clear, consistent story — "Mike's Plumbing, family-owned, residential service in Cherry Hill, NJ, 4.7 stars from 80 reviews, specializing in older homes" — ChatGPT can describe you to a customer. It can also recommend you when the customer asks.

If those sources are sparse, contradictory, or empty — you'll either get skipped, or worse, ChatGPT will mention you with wrong details. (Wrong phone number on your old Yelp page is a common one.)

## The most common reasons businesses are invisible to AI

I've audited a lot of these. The pattern is almost always one of these four:

**1. Google Business Profile is half-empty.** Hours missing or wrong. Services not listed. Address inconsistent with the website. Photos from 2018. AI reads "this business doesn't seem to operate clearly" and skips it.

**2. The website doesn't say what you actually do.** A homepage that says "Welcome to our family of services. We're passionate about excellence." tells AI nothing. AI is looking for facts: what, where, for whom. A simple "We do residential plumbing in Cherry Hill, Marlton, and Voorhees" beats a paragraph of marketing fluff every time.

**3. Reviews are stale or sparse.** Under 15 reviews, or last review from three years ago, reads as "this business may not be active." AI doesn't want to recommend a place that might not exist anymore.

**4. Conflicting info across the web.** Old Yelp page with the wrong number. Old Facebook page with a different address. Old listing on a directory with a name typo. AI sees the inconsistency and treats your information as unreliable.

## What "fixing your picture" actually involves

You don't need to learn anything technical. The fixes are boring and unglamorous:

- Make sure your Google profile is complete, accurate, and recently updated
- Write three or four sentences on your homepage that clearly say what you do, where, for whom
- Clean up old listings on Yelp, Yellow Pages, BBB, Facebook, etc. — make the basics match
- Keep reviews flowing in. Two to six a month, consistently
- Reply to reviews — both good and bad. AI reads your replies

None of these are individually clever. Done together, they make AI describe you the way a customer would: clearly, accurately, with a recommendation.

## Why this matters even if AI is small today

You might say "ChatGPT is still small. Most people use Google." That's true today. It won't be in two years.

Here's the bigger reason to start now: the businesses that look clean and consistent to AI today are the ones that will be recommended *by name* tomorrow, when AI is bigger. AI's picture of you compounds. The longer you look like a real, active, well-run local business in its sources, the more reliably it recommends you.

Businesses that start cleaning up in 2027 will be playing catch-up against the ones who started in 2026.
