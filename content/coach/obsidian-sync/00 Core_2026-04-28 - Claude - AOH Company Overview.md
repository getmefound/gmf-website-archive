## What is AOH?

> Mike's verbatim (Ground Truth, 2026-04-30): *"its a business like in the name that has a hub of ai offerings and service which business dont have to learn, can outsource to us"*

AI Outsource Hub is a service business with a hub of AI offerings that local businesses don't have to learn. They outsource the operation of those AI tools to AOH.

We are not a software company. We are a service business that runs the AI for our clients.

The core promise: **you don't have to figure out the AI tool, hire someone to run it, or babysit it.** We do.

### 3 active pillars + 1 upsell

| | Status | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Reviews** (Starter $99/mo + Pro $199/mo) | Pillar | Email-based (Starter) or full SMS+email + GBP optimization (Pro) |
| **Voice Agent** ($299/mo + $299 setup) | Pillar | Multilingual AI receptionist, 27+ languages, books calls 24/7 |
| **AI Lead Gen** ($249/mo + $99 setup) | Pillar | Cold-outreach engine + curated prospect lists per niche |
| **Website Rebuild** ($149/mo + $499 build) | **Upsell only** — sold to existing AOH clients, never cold-pitched | Modern responsive site, ongoing maintenance |

**Under consideration as candidate 4th pillar (Mike's call):** AI image generation for ads (image-only, narrower than the previously-rejected full ad-creation offer). See `project_aoh_ai_ad_creation.md` for the prior decision and Ground Truth for the current "under consideration" note.

## Who AOH is for

### The 8 niches we focus on in v1

**Mike covers:** pet groomers, funeral homes, moving companies, marketing consultants

**Kip covers:** vets, senior living facilities, auto repair shops, B2B services (broad — tightens once we see what closes)

**Teri** works her existing nursing-home contact book separately (cold-call only, not in email pipeline).

> Niche selection rationale (per Ground Truth): emerged from prior research targeting niches NOT yet saturated by AI / agency cold outreach. This is internal strategic intel — NOT customer-facing pitch.

### ICP filters (adjusts based on weekly Scout performance digest)

**Hard disqualifiers — never pitch, exclude from prospect lists:**

| Filter | Threshold | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Annual revenue | See tiered table below | Marketing budget is ~5-10% of revenue. Below the SKU-specific floor, prospects can't absorb the price without churn risk. |
| Employee count | 1-2 people (solo or solo+1) | They ARE the voice/face/CRM. Voice Agent makes no sense. They can buy Reviews Starter only if revenue qualifies. |
| Already on agency retainer | Visible in Apollo or website footer | Brand allegiance hard to break. Low ROI to target. |
| Outside our 8 niches | Anything not above | v1 expertise locked. Spreading thin = no proof points anywhere. |
| Outside service area | Not local in CT/NoVA, or not remote-friendly B2B | Local-rank value muted, no proximity story. |

### Revenue floor — TIERED by SKU (locked 2026-04-30)

Different SKUs have different minimum-revenue requirements based on the 8%-of-revenue marketing-spend benchmark and SaaS churn-cluster research.

| AOH SKU | Minimum prospect annual revenue |
|---|---|
| Reviews Starter ($79-99/mo) | **$300K** |
| Reviews Pro ($149-199/mo) | **$300K** |
| AI Lead Gen ($179-249/mo) | **$400K** |
| Voice Agent ($349-499/mo) | **$400K** |

**Niche-specific adjustments:**

- **Vets** — $400K minimum (sole-vet rural under $300K is too thin)
- **Funeral homes** — $500K minimum (rural/cremation-only homes can run $400-600K despite $1M industry average)
- **Auto repair (independents)** — $400K minimum
- **Senior living** — not binding (typically multi-million revenue)
- **Pet groomers** — $300K Reviews Starter; $400K for Voice Agent (thinnest niche)
- **Moving / marketing consultants / B2B** — $400K universal

**Why tiered:** Voice Agent at $299/mo consumes 15% of a $300K shop's marketing budget — within healthy SaaS-spend range. Reviews Starter at $99/mo consumes only 5% — comfortable. (Pre-2026-05-13 Voice Agent was $499/mo at 25%, in the SaaS churn-cluster zone.) (Sources: Deloitte CMO 2025, Gartner CMO Spend Survey 2025, SBA, Federal Reserve SBCS, Paddle/Vitally SaaS churn benchmarks.)

### Operational impact

- **Cold outreach (Apollo / Scout):** filter prospects to $300K+ for Reviews Starter pitches; $400K+ for full-stack pitches
- **Sales conversation:** Relay ($299/mo) is safe to pitch at **$300K+ shops** (12.5% of marketing budget — healthy range). Never pitch Relay to sub-$300K shops — churn-cluster zone, will cancel in 60-90 days. Lead with Reviews Starter ($99/mo) for sub-$300K shops.
- **Homepage diagnostic:** Reviews Starter is the low-barrier doorway button (locked 2026-04-29) — only SKU $300K shops can absorb.

### Ideal-fit signals (lean in hard)

- $500K-$5M annual revenue
- 5-30 employees
- Owner-operated or family-owned
- Active Google Business Profile, <50 reviews (room to grow)
- Has a website (even outdated is fine)
- 80%+ of revenue is local (or B2B with national reach for Kip's niches)

## Anti-positioning (who AOH is explicitly NOT for)

> Mike's primary filter (Ground Truth, 2026-04-30): *"i basically want to make sure business have enough money to pay me"*

**The dominant filter is solvency.** Below the revenue floor for the SKU pitched, the prospect is disqualified — period. Everything else is secondary.

**For the website's "Is this for me?" section:**

> AOH is for service businesses that can comfortably absorb $99-499/mo without flinching. If you can't afford that, we're not the right fit yet. If you want to manage every campaign yourself, hire a contractor — we'll be too hands-off for you. If you want to skim a Slack channel each Friday and watch the numbers go up, talk to us.

The right buyer self-identifies. The wrong buyer leaves before wasting your time.

**Secondary anti-positioning (sales-conversation context, not website lead):**
- Solo operators or 1-2 person shops (under our minimum staff threshold)
- Owners who want to manage their own marketing software
- Big agencies with retainer relationships already in place
- Tech-skittish boomers who need an hour to log into Gmail
- Businesses outside our 8 niches in v1
- Anyone expecting "10x ROI in 30 days" guarantees

## Brand voice — locked 2026-04-30 (5 elements)

| Element | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Visual frame** | Soft. Before/after slider as primary mechanic. No fear-mongering imagery. |
| **Tone** | Helpful neighbor, not the doom prophet. |
| **Copy strategy** | Stakes-aware loss framing without scolding. Name the cost the prospect is already paying, offer relief. Don't make them feel bad for it. |
| **Copy style** | Plain language for non-tech buyers. Like explaining technology to a teacher — no jargon, real analogies. Mike's prior-career skill applied to AI. |
| **Hero mechanic** | Before/after slider IS the hero. Visitor sees a relatable business transformed by AI. No tagline-as-positioning. impeccable.style aesthetic reference. |

**Rejected framings — never use:** "AI department," "outsourced AI department," "the AI department your business can't afford to hire," "AI is the voice" (narrows to Voice Agent only), or any framing that pigeonholes AOH as a single product (review-improvement company, voice-agent company, etc.).

## Pricing rules — locked 2026-04-30 (research-backed, asymmetric per tier)

Asymmetric pricing model — different mechanic per tier based on how each offer sells. **For full reasoning + sources, see [[AOH Ground Truth]] pricing section.**

| Offer | Public price | How intro pricing works |
|---|---|---|
| Reviews Starter | $99/mo, $0 setup | **Public intro: $79/mo locked-for-life** if claimed within 14 days of cold-email send. Drives impulse-buy via Stripe checkout. |
| Reviews Pro | $199/mo + $199 setup | Public price firm. Intro pricing for early clients per discussion with management — not advertised, sales-call lever only. |
| Voice Agent | $299/mo + $299 setup | Same — public price firm; intro per Mike's discretion. (Repriced 2026-05-13 from $499/$499.) |
| AI Lead Gen | $249/mo + $99 setup | Same — public price firm; intro per Mike's discretion. |
| Website Rebuild | $149/mo + $499 build | Same — public price firm; intro per Mike's discretion. (Sold as upsell to existing clients only.) |

**Critical rule:** Specific intro percentages / discount amounts are NEVER documented anywhere — neither publicly nor in this Company Overview nor in the per-offer docs. Reps say *"let me check with Mike — we have some flexibility for early clients"* — they NEVER quote a specific discount without Mike's approval.

**Voice Agent paid add-ons:** Voice clone +$49/mo, extra language +$29/mo (these are public add-ons, not internal intro pricing).

**Mandatory contract clauses (protect unit economics on locked rates):**

1. **Voice Agent: 500-min/mo cap, $0.49/min overage.** Voice-platform cost ~$0.163/min — uncapped chatty client = lifetime margin destroyer.
2. **AI Lead Gen: niche-pooling required when intro pricing is offered.** Pool research seats across 3-5 intro-priced clients in same niche, NOT dedicated seats per client.
3. **Reviews Pro: 100 review-asks/mo cap when intro pricing is offered, $0.05/ask overage.** SMS + A2P fees scale with volume.

**Pricing rules:**

1. **Founding-client pricing is a sales lever, never advertised publicly.** Website shows locked targets only.
2. **Locked-for-life:** once a founding client signs, their price grandfathers forever. They never get raised.
3. **Step-up trigger:** after 5 paying clients in an offer, that offer's price moves UP toward locked target for new prospects.
4. **Full locked pricing** kicks in after 10 paying clients across all offers.
5. **Add-ons start low** ($29-49/mo) to seed adoption — better to have 50% of clients buying 1 add-on at $49 than 10% buying it at $99.

### The pricing philosophy — what Coach + reps quote (Mike's verbatim, 2026-04-30)

When a prospect asks "why are you offering me this lower founding-client price? what's the catch?":

> **"Honest answer — we're just starting to offer these services locally. Rather than ask for your business, we'd like to earn it. So you get our founding-client price — locked for as long as you stay with us."**

This works because: admits new without saying "zero customers" + flips the typical agency dynamic (earn vs ask) + closes with concrete economic benefit (locked price).

## About-page structure (locked 2026-04-30, expert-backed)

Full structure is in [[AOH Ground Truth]]. Summary: **9-element pattern-recognition career-arc** (NOT dramatic origin moment). Sources: Wiebe / StoryBrand / CXL / Sheridan / NN/g / Unbounce.

Element order:
1. Hero block (outcome headline + Mike photo + 1-line positioning)
2. Opening hook (the problem in customer's words)
3. Founder story — pattern-recognition arc ("After 15 years selling to local SMB owners, I kept watching the same things break...")
4. What AOH does differently (mechanism, 4 pillars summary)
5. Founding-client framing (replaces testimonials)
