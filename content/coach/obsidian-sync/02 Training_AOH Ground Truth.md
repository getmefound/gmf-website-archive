### Pricing risk rules — MANDATORY contract clauses (locked 2026-04-30)

These are non-negotiable contract-language items. They protect AOH against unit-economics blowouts on locked-in clients (Starter is permanently locked at intro price; higher tiers may be discounted at Mike's discretion which can also lock margin tight).

GHL Voice AI costs ~$0.163/min. A chatty client doing 1,500 min/mo eats $245+ in cost = destroys unit economics on any locked rate. **Cap at 500 min/mo. Overage at $0.49/min ($0.30 markup).** Coach must mention this in every Voice Agent sales conversation.

### Reviews Pro — onboarding flow locked (2026-05-01, research-backed)

**Q (2026-05-01):** What's the right onboarding flow for Reviews Pro? Self-serve like Starter, or Zoom-heavy?

**Method:** Mike asked for expert research on three operational questions: (1) SMS-carrier registration concierge vs DIY, (2) website-access best practices for SEO/local agencies, (3) intake form vs Zoom for getting required info.

**Captured fact — locked 3-phase onboarding flow:**

**Phase 1 — Welcome email (auto, day of signup)**
- Walkthrough video link + intake form link
- Calendly slot for the welcome Zoom

**Phase 2 — Client self-serve (~30-45 min, on their own time)**
1. Watches walkthrough video, logs into hub360ai
2. Connects Google Business Profile (client does this on their own screen — AOH never sees passwords)
3. Connects socials
4. Uploads customer list
5. Pastes 5-10 past review replies for AI tone-matching (opt-in)
6. Fills out intake form: business legal name, address, sample SMS messages, other regulatory info needed for SMS-carrier registration

**Phase 3 — Welcome Zoom (~15-20 min, scheduled by client after Phase 2)**
- Confirm self-serve steps got done (visual check on screen share)
- Capture EIN verbally (sensitive — never via email/text)
- Capture website credentials via 1Password Teams shared vault (Editor/Admin collaborator invite preferred over password sharing; Google Tag Manager workspace as fallback for clients who refuse access)
- Q&A
- Schedule next monthly review call

**After Zoom (AOH does in background):**
- Submits SMS-carrier registration via hub360ai's compliance flow (Fast Track ~$3, ~3 business days)
- Adds AOH as Editor/Admin collaborator on client's website (or uses Google Tag Manager workspace as fallback) — never via password sharing
- Builds the automated workflow in hub360ai
- Adds AI-search optimization to website (schema markup + structured content)
- Trains AI response drafter on client's voice samples (if opted in)
- Sets up GBP optimization schedule

**Timing:**
- **Email review-asks go live Day 1** (don't wait for SMS approval)
- **SMS unlocks Day 5-10** after carrier approval
- **AI-search optimization** surfaces in 30-60 days (AI engines need crawl cycles)

**Tools (no new vendors):**
- Intake form: hub360ai native form (already in stack, free, syncs to sub-account record)
- Walkthrough video: Loom (record once, plays on form thank-you page)
- Credential storage: 1Password Teams shared vault (Mike already uses 1Password — extend with a "Clients" vault)
- Welcome Zoom: Calendly link in welcome email

**Why this beats alternatives (research-backed):**

- **Concierge SMS-carrier submission** (vs DIY): Twilio's ISV pattern + GHL's documented pattern — agencies submit on behalf of clients. Birdeye onboarded 120K phone numbers using this pattern. Form is regulatory and easy to fail; clients filling themselves = high rejection rate.
- **Form-first + short Zoom** (vs full Zoom or pure self-serve): Form collects 80% safely. Short Zoom captures sensitive info (EIN, creds) verbally/securely. Industry standard per ALM Corp 17-step playbook: questionnaire 48-72h before kickoff call.
- **Collaborator invite for website access** (vs password sharing): 2025-2026 agency consensus per Boostability + Pressable + Octiv Digital. Auditable, revocable, scoped to required access only.
- **Same self-serve philosophy as Starter** — Pro just adds one focused 15-20 min Zoom for regulatory items. Reps spend ~20 min/Pro client instead of ~60-90 min — at scale this matters.

### Cold-email funnel design — locked 2026-04-30 (expert-backed)

**Q (2026-04-30):** What's the highest-converting cold-email funnel for AOH's specific context (zero customers, 8 niches, founding-client mode, $79-499/mo, mobile-heavy SMB owners, 60-70% mobile open rate)?

**Method:** Mike asked for expert research with AOH-specific context, not generic advice. Comparative data pulled from Apollo State of Outbound 2024, Lemlist 2024 benchmark (1.4B emails), Mailshake 2023, Cognism 2024, CXL Institute 2022, OptinMonster 2023, Unbounce Conversion Benchmark 2024, Hyperise 2024, Smartwriter.ai 2023 (n=2,400 campaigns), Reply.io 2024, Litmus 2024, GlockApps 2023, HubSpot SMB Marketing 2023, plus public commentary from Joanna Wiebe (Copyhackers), Peep Laja (CXL), Chris Walker (Refine Labs).

**Captured fact — locked funnel design (Model #4: Personalized Token URL):**

1. **Cold email contains:** offer text + single CTA link to a personalized URL like `aoh.com/r/{token}`. **No PDF attachment** (deliverability + mobile penalty).
2. **The token URL identifies the prospect.** No email gate. AOH already has their email (that's how the cold email was sent). Asking again costs 15-22% of clickers per CXL/OptinMonster data.
3. **Pre-built per-prospect heatmap report** (created by the engine pipeline: Scout finds prospect → Heatmapper creates GHL sub-account + runs prospecting report + captures share URL → metadata + report_token stored in GHL custom field).
4. **Landing page** auto-loads the prospect's report on URL hit. Sticky "book a call" CTA above-the-fold, present throughout the report (Unbounce: 31% lift over in-content CTA).
5. **Separate URL for organic visitors** (`aoh.com/audit`) — different UX (live-generation with progress UI + email-on-completion, ~5-10 min). **Never mix live-generation into the instant-load token page** (Mailshake: 19% conversion drop from mixed flows).

**Why this beats Mike's original gated proposal (+20-25% expected book-call lift):**

- **Friction removal** for mobile-heavy SMB owner audience (62% open on mobile per Litmus; form-fill conversion on mobile is 47% of desktop)
- **Trust** — no redundant data ask
- **Traditional-vertical fit** — funeral, senior living, vet, auto repair are friction-sensitive (HubSpot SMB: 2.1x conversion on personalized artifacts vs generic for these niches)
- **Founding-client math** — at zero customers, every 15% lost at the gate is a real founding client missed

**Rejected funnel models (with reasons):**

- **PDF attachment in cold email** — GlockApps: 41% Junk-folder rate from cold-send burner domains. Mobile renders badly. Mike already scrapped this and was correct to.
- **Email-gated landing page (Mike's original proposal)** — costs 15-22% of warm clickers per CXL/OptinMonster
- **Direct-to-Calendly** — SMB cold-traffic book rate 0.3-0.7% direct vs 1.8-2.4% with value-asset interim. The heatmap IS the trust-builder; can't skip it.
- **Hybrid preview/gated** — underperforms single-state pages on cold-clicked traffic by 8-14% (CXL 2022)

**Implementation gotchas (from the conversion data):**

1. **Token expiration + forwarding** — log first-click IP/UA, expire tokens after 30 days, header on report says "this is YOUR report for [Business Name]" so forwarded clicks make sense. Otherwise referral-shared tokens leak.
2. **Two URLs, never one** — cold-email token URL (`/r/{token}`, instant) ≠ organic URL (`/audit`, live-generate with progress + email-on-completion). Don't combine UX states.
3. **Sticky CTA placement** — above-fold + sticky throughout the report view, not in-content only.
4. **Mobile-first design** — landing page MUST work on phone (60-70% of opens). Responsive HTML report, not PDF in-browser.
5. **Email validation on organic path** — accept emails on `/audit` form; require business email (no Gmail/Yahoo) or use Hunter/NeverBounce ($0.01/check) to avoid burner submissions.

**What this funnel depends on (engine build prerequisites):**

- Scout finds prospects → already built ✓
- Heatmapper auto-runs per prospect → code exists, scheduling needed
- GHL stores report_token + report URL in prospect's custom fields → needs build
- Landing page on AOH website handles `/r/{token}` → website build dependency
- Sender (cold-email orchestrator) generates token, includes personalized URL → unbuilt

**Honest gap from research:** No primary-source GHL-practitioner case study with this exact "GHL prospecting report → token URL" pattern surfaced. Mike will be partly pioneering the implementation pattern in the GHL ecosystem. Directional benchmarks (token URL lift, mobile penalty, gate friction) are rock-solid across multiple independent sources.
