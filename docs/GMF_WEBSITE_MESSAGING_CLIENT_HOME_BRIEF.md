# GMF Website Messaging Brief: Reports + Client Home Page

Status: active website copy brief
Owner: Mike Egidio
Use: give this to Claude when designing or rewriting website sections about what clients get.

## Core Message

GetMeFound is not selling another dashboard.

GMF gives local business owners a simple done-for-you visibility system:

- we check what Google and AI can understand
- we fix the public signals that make the business easier to trust and recommend
- we keep reviews and profile activity moving
- we show proof in a private client home page

Use plain owner language. Do not say AEO, GEO, entity optimization, workflow, agent chain, heatmap, internal queue, or subaccount unless Mike explicitly asks.

## Public Website Framing

### Short Version

You get a private client home page where you can see what is done, what is next, what we still need from you, and the proof that your visibility work is moving.

No software to learn. No dashboard to manage. GMF does the work and shows the receipts.

### Longer Version

Every client gets a simple private page for their business. It shows the current plan, setup status, visibility reports, review activity, Google profile work, client uploads, and anything waiting for approval.

The owner should never wonder:

- what are they doing?
- what do they need from me?
- did anything happen this month?
- what should I fix next?

The client home page answers those questions in plain English.

## Prospect Free Visibility Report

Purpose: help the owner understand the gap and start Get Found.

The free report should feel useful but not like a full paid audit.

What the prospect sees:

- visibility score
- local competitor comparison
- 3-5 plain signals Google and AI can understand
- one primary gap
- locked fixes available with Get Found

Sample website wording:

```text
Your free visibility check shows how clearly Google and AI can understand your business compared with nearby competitors.

You will see your score, the biggest public gaps, and the first fixes we would make. The full baseline and before/after proof unlock after signup.
```

Avoid promising rankings. Avoid giving a detailed DIY checklist before signup.

## Client Home Page

Purpose: prove GMF is working and reduce owner anxiety.

The client page should feel like a calm operating room for the business, not software.

First screen should show:

- business name and logo
- current plan
- current status
- one plain-English summary
- one next action if the client needs to do something
- quick proof counters
- upload/update button when needed

Client page sections:

- Today: what changed recently
- Needed from you: access, approvals, uploads, missing business facts
- Visibility: score, baseline, competitor gap, latest report
- Reviews: requests sent, new reviews, private feedback, held-back rows
- Google profile: access, fixes, posts, services, photos, drift checks
- Reports: baseline, before/after proof, monthly recap
- Approvals: reply drafts, SMS readiness, profile edits, public-facing changes
- Uploads: customer lists, logo/assets, do-not-contact notes, service updates
- Unlocked next: useful greyed-out upgrades available with Stay Found or Always Ready

Website wording:

```text
After signup, your business gets a private client home page.

It shows what we are doing, what is finished, what is waiting on you, and the proof behind each monthly update. You can upload customer lists, approve changes, view reports, and see what is unlocked next without learning another marketing tool.
```

## Offer-Specific What They Get

### Get Found

Get Found is the foundation setup.

Client receives:

- baseline visibility check
- Google profile cleanup plan
- website/profile fact sync
- review link capture
- first review request path
- before/after snapshot
- private client home page

Website wording:

```text
Get Found fixes the foundation. We clean up the public signals Google and AI already look at, then show you the before/after proof in your private client page.
```

### Stay Found

Stay Found keeps the business active and current.

Client receives:

- recurring review request path
- customer/job upload area
- monthly visibility report
- Google profile freshness checks
- review activity recap
- private feedback routing
- client home page updates

Website wording:

```text
Stay Found keeps your profile from going quiet. Each month, we keep review activity moving, watch for visibility drift, and send a plain-English recap so you know what changed.
```

### Always Ready

Always Ready is the deeper visibility and readiness layer.

Client receives:

- deeper AI/search visibility checks
- competitor movement notes
- content and service clarity opportunities
- guarded review reply drafts when approved
- voice/readiness workflows only when approved
- strategy notes inside the client page

Website wording:

```text
Always Ready is for businesses that want the deeper layer: AI visibility, competitor movement, answer-ready content, and guarded automation readiness. Nothing public-facing goes live without approval.
```

## Locked Preview Wording

Locked previews should sell the next step without feeling pushy.

Good wording:

```text
Available after Get Found
Monthly visibility checks, recurring review requests, and deeper AI answer monitoring can be added once the foundation is clean.
```

```text
Locked until approved
Review replies and voice/readiness features stay approval-gated. You stay in control of anything public-facing.
```

Avoid:

- aggressive upsell language
- scary AI claims
- ranking guarantees
- internal tool names
- "our agents will..."

## Voice And Tone

Use:

- plain English
- calm confidence
- "we handle it"
- "you see proof"
- "only act when we need you"
- "no dashboard to manage"

Avoid:

- buzzwords
- technical SEO jargon
- overexplaining the workflow
- making the owner feel behind or foolish
- making the product sound like SaaS
