# Google Business Profile Access And Update Test

Status: client-zero test
Owner: Local Visibility Manager
Reviewer: Manager
Last updated: 2026-05-21

## Purpose

Use AOH's own Google Business Profile as the first test before asking clients to give us access.

This proves the handoff is simple:

1. The business owner invites AOH by email.
2. AOH accepts access without asking for the owner's password.
3. Local Visibility Manager confirms what can be updated.
4. AOH prepares the first profile update.
5. Public changes are approved before posting.

## Owner

Local Visibility Manager owns Google Business Profile access, profile health, review links, posts, photos, services, and local visibility checks.

Manager owns the brief and blocker tracking.

GHL Expert helps only when GBP needs to connect into HighLevel/reputation workflows.

## Recommended Client Access

Default role: **Manager**.

Use Manager access for normal client work because it can handle daily profile operations without giving AOH user-admin control.

Use Owner access only when AOH truly needs to add/remove users, transfer access, or handle ownership/admin work. That should be a separate approval.

No password sharing.

## Client Invite Instructions

Ask the client to:

1. Go to their Business Profile.
2. Open **Business Profile settings**.
3. Open **People and access**.
4. Select **Add**.
5. Enter the AOH Google email.
6. Choose **Manager** unless AOH specifically requested Owner.
7. Select **Invite**.

Then AOH accepts the invite from that Google account.

## AOH Test Steps

1. Pick the AOH Google email clients should invite.
2. Add that email to AOH's own Business Profile first.
3. Confirm the invite arrives and can be accepted.
4. Confirm Local Visibility Manager can see the profile.
5. Check the basics: name, address/service area, phone, website, hours, categories, services, photos, posts, review link, and unanswered reviews.
6. Draft one safe GBP update for AOH.
7. Get Mike approval before publishing the update.
8. Log what worked, what was confusing, and what screenshots/video are needed for clients.

## Client-Safe Explanation

Use this language:

> We do not need your Google password. You add our business email as a Manager on your Google Business Profile. That lets us help with profile updates, posts, photos, review links, and visibility checks while you stay in control.

## Source

Google Business Profile help says owners can invite additional owners or managers, each user should use their own Google Account, managers can edit profile info, respond to reviews, and manage posts, and the invite path is Business Profile settings -> People and access -> Add -> enter email -> choose Owner or Manager -> Invite.

Source: https://support.google.com/business/answer/3403100
