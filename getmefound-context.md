## Recurring Tiers (GetMeFound.ai)

### Tier 1 — Stay Found
- **Price:** $99/mo, no contract
- **Channel:** Email review requests only (no SMS at this tier)
- **Why email only:** A2P SMS registration takes 1–4 weeks per client and costs $48+ setup per client. Email still generates 15 reviews per 100 requests. SMS is the upgrade incentive.
- **Deliverables:**
  - Weekly client list upload → automated email review requests
  - 1 weekly GBP post (keeps AI search signal fresh)
  - Free website hosting for GMF-managed sites
  - Review monitoring across platforms
  - Monthly 1-page visibility report
- **Your hard cost:** ~$20–30/mo per client after hosting/tooling
- **Gross margin:** ~60–70%
- **Competitor anchor:** NiceJob charges $75/mo for software only. You do it for them at $99 and include website hosting.

### Tier 2 — Review Power
- **Price:** $149/mo, no contract
- **Channel:** SMS + email combined
- **Why SMS here:** SMS converts to Google reviews at 3–5x the rate of email (TrueReview data). This is the performance-based upsell argument.
- **A2P handled by you** — $48 one-time brand registration + ~$10/mo per client. You absorb and manage this.
- **Deliverables (everything in Stay Found plus):**
  - SMS review request campaigns (not just weekly — triggered)
  - AI response drafted in their brand voice — they approve before posting
  - Negative review alert + suggested response within 4 hours
  - Monthly review sentiment report
  - Perplexity + Google AI Overview citation check monthly
- **Your hard cost:** ~$45–55/mo per client
- **Gross margin:** ~63–70%
- **Competitor kill shot:** Birdeye $299–$449/mo annual contract. Podium $399–$599/mo annual contract. Both have documented cancellation nightmares. You: $149/mo, cancel any time.

### Tier 3 — AI Ready Bundle
- **Price:** $299/mo, no contract
- **Deliverables (everything in Review Power plus):**
  - AI voice agent trained on their business, services, pricing, hours
  - Ready when Google's AI calls to check availability (live as of May 2026)
  - Handles inbound AI agent inquiries 24/7
  - Full GBP content management (beyond weekly post — location pages, FAQ schema, local content)
  - Monthly 30-min strategy call
  - AEO check — are they being cited in AI search answers?
- **Your hard cost:** ~$90–110/mo per client
- **Gross margin:** ~63–70%
- **Urgency pitch:** "Google's AI calling feature launched this week. It calls your business to check pricing for customers. If no one answers or it gets your info wrong, you lose the booking."

## Key Data Points (cite these in sales conversations)

- **Harvard Business School:** 1-star rating increase = 5–9% revenue increase. For a $300k/year business = $15,000–$27,000 added revenue.
- **Womply (200k+ US businesses):** Businesses responding to 25%+ of reviews earn 35% more revenue. Businesses with 9+ fresh reviews (last 90 days) earn 52% more.
- **SMS vs email review requests:** SMS converts at 3–5x the rate of email. SMS: 20 reviews per 100 requests. Email: 15 reviews per 100 requests. (GatherUp platform data)
- **Reviews are now AI ranking signals:** Perplexity cites reviews in 100% of responses. Google AI Overviews cite review platforms in 34.5% of responses.
- **Google IO May 2026:** AI calling feature for local businesses is live and rolling out nationally. Businesses need to be ready.
- **BrightLocal 2026:** 97% of consumers read reviews before purchasing. Businesses below 4.0 stars lose majority of potential customers before any other factor.
- **Small business marketing spend:** Average SMB spends $2,083/mo on digital marketing (DesignRush 2026). Your top tier at $299/mo is 14% of that.

## A2P SMS — What You Need to Know

- Required for all business SMS in the US since Feb 2025. Carriers block unregistered traffic.
- **Cost per client:** ~$48 one-time brand registration + $15–17 campaign registration + $1.50–10/mo ongoing + $0.003–0.005 per message
- **Timeline:** 1–4 weeks for approval
- **Your decision:** Only offer SMS at Review Power tier ($149/mo) and above. Stay Found uses email only.
- You handle A2P registration for clients — this is a differentiator. NiceJob and TrueReview don't do this.
